About

I'm Barney. I'm a programmer and data junkie. I trust you to be every bit as smart as you are. I'm not much for babying; I'd rather design for intent.

COVID-19 and population data from the COVID-19 Data Repository by the Center for Systems Science and Engineering (CSSE) at Johns Hopkins University.

You can drop me a line at c19d@barneyb.com if you'd like to get in touch. Or call me, though you'll need to your own digging for that one. If you find my Twitter account, don't expect a response. Any likely matches on other social networks are false.

All data are updated daily, about 5:20am UTC, including any historical corrections. That's CSSE's schedule, plus a handful of minutes on my end to hopefully avoid missing updates.

Weekly figures are labeled by their final day, anchored to today. If you come back tomorrow, everything will be shifted forward one day. There's no way to get a "weeks starting Sunday" (or whatever day you wish) sort of view; they are always anchored to today with rolling week boundaries.

Series with "Rate" in their name are per day per 100K population. Even when looking at a weekly view. When you want to compare a daily figure against a weekly table, you'll thank me for the table showing a daily average over the week, instead of seven times that figure.

The tech stack is very simple:

  1. CSSE's repo of CSV files
  2. a Java utility aggregates, indexes, and transforms the data
  3. a JS client presents the data

Parts two and three are available at https://github.com/barneyb/covid-report if you want source. The Java binary you'll have to build yourself. There is no build process for the client. The Java code uses Spring, Jackson, Lombok, and OpenCSV. The client code is entirely mine.

Note that if you go poking in the code, I have deliberately not looked to following reasonable development practices. This is a personal project, and primarily about playing with the code. Finding the pain points that drive the best practices in a toy project is exactly the sort of thing. This is also the first time I'd ever used Lombok or Java's new-ish functional pieces, as I've always looked to Groovy for similar features (along with its meta-programming voodoo).

All of the infrastructure pieces are also present in the Github repository, aside from the cron config: 

    17 21 * * * $COVID_ROOT/hopkins/refresh.sh

Yes, I use the cron email as notification of new data. It works. Yes, I run my box on my local time, not UTC. That one is less graceful. I am hoping the whole pandemic thing will be over before fall's DST transition.
